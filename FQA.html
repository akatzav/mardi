What is Mardi Gras?
Mardi Gras is a holiday that originates from the Christian tradition, which is celebrated in many Christian communities around the world, as part of the carnival celebrations.
There are places where Mardi Gras has turned from a religious holiday into a secular cultural festival, which includes street parades full of joy.

What is the true meaning of Mardi Gras?
Mardi Gras, (French: Fat Tuesday) festive day celebrated
in France on Shrove Tuesday (the Tuesday before Ash Wednesday), which marks the close of the pre-Lenten season.
The French name Mardi Gras means Fat Tuesday,
from the custom of using all the fats in the home before Lent in preparation for fasting and abstinence.
